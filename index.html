Historical Tapestry: Historically known as Ekachakrapura in the Dwapara-yuga, Kaiwara is the epic ground where the Pandavas lived incognito during exile, and where the mighty Bhima defeated the demon Bakasura. In modern times, it is revered as the divine seat of saint-poet Yogi Kaiwara Narayana Taatha.

Major Attractions

What makes Kaiwara a perfect day-trip escape from the city.

Sri Amara Narayana Temple

A breathtaking temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, closely connected with the history of Satya-yuga and later beautifully constructed/renovated during the Vijayanagara Empire. Features incredibly intricate granite stone pillar carvings.

Yogi Narayana Ashram

An ashram dedicated to the great bilingual poet-saint Thataiyya (1726–1836). It remains a focal point for spiritual, religious, and philanthropic work where free mass meals are served daily to visitors.

Kaiwara Betta (Trek)

Managed safely by the Forest Department (bookable on MyEcoTrip), this moderately challenging rock trail climbs past massive granite boulders up to the spectacular Mylapurabetta Viewpoint. Great for nature lovers.

Bhima Bakasura Hill & Cave

A rocky hill containing the historical Bheemalingeshwara temple. Mythological lore asserts that a cave here holds the remnants of the demon Bakasura, adding an intriguing atmosphere for history buffs.

Kailasha Giri Cave Temples

Located roughly 7 km away from the main town, this hill is famous for its massive cave temples carved right out of rocks, containing shrines like Ambaji Durga and Chenna Keshava.

Lakshmana Tirtha

Situated atop the hill, local lore says Lakshmana shot an arrow into the ground to fetch water to quench Sita's thirst, forming a pristine, historic pool alongside a temple to Chamundeshwari Devi.

Best Time to Visit Early morning (October to March) to beat the scorching afternoon sun on the rocks


⚠️ Tips for a Safe & Impactful Visit

  • Monkeys Ahead: Watch your backpacks, food items, and gadgets safely as local monkeys are very active.
  • Strictly No Littering: Carry a small garbage collection bag with you to protect the eco-sensitive forest reserves.
  • Footwear Matters: Wear strong trekking shoes with solid grip; granite boulders get extremely slick after rain showers.
  • Timing Strategy: Try to scale the hills before 11:00 AM as the bare rocks capture and radiate intense thermal heat.
